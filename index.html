I met loneliness
on a rainy day,

His presence
loomed
over
mine,

Soul, thousand miles away.

We exchanged silence,
He never looked me in the face.

Have I seen him before?

(I'd recognize his gaze anywhere.)

Though I never believed in wishes anymore.

He sat in the passenger seat,

Breath fogging the glass.

Long drives made the world turn blurry.

He went to my funeral,

Staring as I got buried.

Like he wished I had stayed longer.

But what's the point?
